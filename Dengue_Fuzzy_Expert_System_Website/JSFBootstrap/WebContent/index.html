About Dengue

Dengue is a disease caused by dengue virus that is spread through Aedes mosquitoes. It is seen as a common disease as it can be spread through easily by getting one bite from Aedes mosquitoes. Dengue can cause death if the patient doesn’t get a good treatment. There is no vaccine or cure for dengue fever until now.

Data Analysis

According to the World Health Organization(WHO) updated dengue status in Malaysia, a total of 1,040 cases of dengue were reported during Week 44, 2020, taking the overall number of cases reported to 82,753 as of 31 October 2020.

Types

There are two main forms of dengue disease, dengue fever and the more severe dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF).

Symptoms

There are lots of symptoms of dengue such as nausea, vomiting, rash, aches and pains. It is a very important issue to solve as this disease may cause death.

How is it treated?

There is no medicine for treating dengue fever. Mild cases may be treated at home with rest and plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration. However, for dengue hemorrhagic fever, the more serious form of dengue fever, usually requires treatment in a hospital.

Dengue Test

Dengue can be life-threatening if proper measures are not taken on time. When you get back from a tropical region and see symptoms like headache, high fever, it is wise to take dengue fever test immediately.

Aches and Pains

Eye pain, typically behind the eyes, muscle, joint, or bone pain.

Fever Days

Dengue fever usually occurs after an incubation period of 4-10 days after the bite of the infected mosquito.

Hemorrhagic Manifestation

The most common hemorrhagic manifestations are mild and include a positive tourniquet test, skin hemorrhages (petechiae, hematomas), epistaxis (nose bleed), gingival bleeding (gum bleed), and microscopic hematuria.

Rash

Rash in dengue fever is a maculopapular or macular confluent rash over the face, thorax, and flexor surfaces, with islands of skin sparing. The rash typically begins on day 3 and persists 2-3 days.

Dengue Disease Self-Checker

The Dengue Disease Self-Checker is an interactive clinical assessment tool that will assist individuals ages 13 and older, and parents and caregivers of children ages 2 to 12 on deciding when to seek testing or medical care if they suspect themselves or someone they know has symptoms of dengue.
